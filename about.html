About Me

This is what I do.

I have had a passion for building things since I was very young. My father was an inventor of sorts, making sports products and selling them through his business, so I had access to a garage full of woodworking tools throughout my early childhood. In this garage, I started crafting toys for myself (mostly wooden swords) and began to develop build, design, and problem solving skills.

I took some woodworking classes in middle school, where I solidified my woodworking background and learned more advanced machining. Inspired by the knowledge I had gained in class and by a developing interest in fishing, I started my first "real" project - a wooden boat. The boat building process significantly increased the breadth of my build skills as well as my confidence, which I used to catapult myself into robotics my freshman year of high school.

In robotics, I had the opportunity to learn metal working. I learned all the manual machines and practiced enough over the offseason to feel good enough to embark on my next big project, the desk go-kart. Momentum from the go-kart carried me through the board track racer, and eventually the frame swap. While most of what I do now is metal fabrication, I occasionally return to my roots as a woodworker, and build things like my scrap table. I have come a long way since the days of carving wooden weaponry, and I have posted most of my bigger projects on this site with the hope that you find my build processes as informative as I have.
